Bay Area Black Youth Tech-a-thon aspires to inform, inspire and bolster opportunities for students and parents of colors in the Silicon Valley and S.F. Bay Area technology sectors

 

March 20, 2017: Santa Clara – Snapchat, Instagram, Tumblr, YouTube, Facebook and LinkedIn - our young people and their parents are naturally conversant with these technologies and other online platforms and video games. “But comfort with computer technology isn't enough to get recruited or hired for a high tech job in Northern California” as Carl Davis Jr, states. “It takes a quality education, technical training and a network of insiders to get connected and employed.

 

On September 30, 2017, nearly 1,000 Black Youth, 8 -24 years of age and their parents, from Northern California will be exposed to science, engineering, mathematics, computer science and computer programming through code writing, engineering competition, hackathon, cyber security seminars, VR demonstrations and STEAM showcases.

 

Chris Norwood, Founder of Bay Area Tutoring Association and Codewritingkids.com shares the sentiment of  Carl Davis Jr., through the lens of education. “Black and Brown students and their parents are primarily technology consumers, not producers in the Silicon Valley. This is reflected in our education systems by the low enrollment in the sciences, AP courses and engagement in STEM related activities. These types of events for students and their parents are a bridge to increasing the Silicon Valley and S.F. Bay Area diversity pipeline.”
